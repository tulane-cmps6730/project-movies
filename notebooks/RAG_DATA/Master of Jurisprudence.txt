Master of Jurisprudence Overview Programs The Master of Jurisprudence (M.J.) is a post-baccalaureate degree that allows non-legal professionals to enhance career related skills through the study of the laws, governmental policy, and the legal system. This degree is designed to infuse career based knowledge with legal education. The program requires the completion of 30 credit hours, and typically takes two years to complete.
