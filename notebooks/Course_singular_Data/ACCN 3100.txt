ACCN 3100Intermediate Accounting I(3)Course focuses on understanding how firms’ operating, financing and investing activities are recorded and disclosed in the financial statements, with an emphasis on assets and their related accounts. Throughout this course, students develop the skills required to prepare and analyze corporate financial statements, as well as an understanding of how managerial incentives interplay with financial reporting. The course is appropriate for accounting and finance students, as well as students of other majors who are interested in acquiring an in-depth understanding of firms’ performance using financial accounting information. (Restriction: Sophomore Standing or above.)Prerequisite(s):ACCN 2010,FINE 3010*andMGSC 3010.*May be taken concurrently.
